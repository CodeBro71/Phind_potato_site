Great Irish Potato Famine

Overview

The Great Irish Potato Famine was a devastating famine that occurred in Ireland between 1845 and 1852. It was caused by a blight that destroyed the potato crop, which was the staple food of the Irish people.

Causes

The famine was caused by a combination of factors including the failure of the potato crop due to the blight, the economic policies of the British government, and the lack of preparedness for such a disaster.

Impact

The famine resulted in the death of approximately 1 million people, making it one of the deadliest famines in history. It also led to mass emigration, with many Irish people moving to the United States and other countries.
